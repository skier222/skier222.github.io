How to use the Skier Massage™

The Skier Massage ™ is most effective when applied directly on bare skin or over a very thin layer of clothing (a t-shirt, long underwear, Spandex, a ski racing suit, etc.).

Liberate your IT Band

When using the Skier Massage ™, relax the affected area by removing as much load on one's muscles as possible. Eliminating any muscle contraction will enable the Skier Massage™ to penetrate deeply into muscle tissue where trigger points frequently hide. This can be accomplished by sitting down on the floor, sitting a chair, or, if applicable, extending your foot to a bench, stool, etc.

Un-Glue your Glutes

The Skier Massage ™ must be firmly gripped with two hands on the soft, ergonomic ski pole-like handles. The amount of downward pressure to apply to the focus muscle group is entirely up to the user, but one should strive to experience a moderate level of discomfort. Use less force if you encounter any significant pain. Limit use to muscle tissue and avoid passing over any bone structure such as your shins and ankles.

Pacify your Hamstrings

For each therapy session with the Skier Massage™, aim for multiple rounds with 15 to 25 passes per round over the impacted area. The number of rounds needed to be effective is up to the user, but attempt at least 3 to 4 per therapy session.

Rubdown Your Calves

Even after multiple good-effort therapy sessions, some trigger points often will need revisiting. These stubborn knots will require daily maintenance therapy to keep them from returning. Over time, you should be able to increase the amount of force that you are able to apply with the Skier Massage ™, while still remaining in the targeted moderate discomfort-zone. Eventually these knots will disappear or decrease in size.

Eliminate Ski Boot Drama

"The best part of skiing is taking off your ski boots"
No doubt, even today some ski boots are excruciatingly painful to get off. Removing those stiff plastic pieces of equipment hardened by the cold can sometimes results in a painful battle between boots and skier. The Skier Massage™ can assuage most ski boot inflicted discomfort in just minutes after coming off the slopes. Take off those clunky boots at lunch and bring out the Skier Massage™ for a mid-day reprieve on the mountain.

Where to use:

  • The bottom of your feet
  • Quadriceps
  • Gluteal muscles (your butt!)
  • Calves
  • IT bands
  • Hamstrings
  • Even the lumbar/lower back area!

Although intended for myofascial therapy on the lower extremities, the Skier Massage™ may also be used in areas of one's upper body.
